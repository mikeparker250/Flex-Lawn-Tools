## NEW SECTION — FUNGICIDE ROTATION REFERENCE

### CHANGE 32 — ADD FUNGICIDE ROTATION CHART

Create a new reference section titled:

> **🔬 Fungicide Rotation Program — Why It Matters**
>
> Using the same fungicide FRAC group back-to-back allows fungal diseases to develop resistance. Once resistance develops, the product stops working. Rotating FRAC groups prevents this.
>
> **Flex Fungicide Rotation:**
>
> Round 3 (Apr 15–May 31): Myclobutanil — FRAC Group 3 — conditional (disease risk only)
> Round 4 (Jun 1–Jul 15): Artavia SC (Azoxystrobin) — FRAC Group 11 — every lawn
> Round 5 (Aug 1–Sep 15): Artavia SC (FRAC 11) + Propiconazole 14.3 (FRAC 3) — every lawn
> Round 7 (Oct 16–Nov 30): Artavia SC (Azoxystrobin) — FRAC Group 11 — warm-season (SDS prevention)
>
> **FRAC Group 11 (QoI / Strobilurin):** Artavia SC, Heritage TL
> — Broad spectrum, systemic, excellent on brown patch and dollar spot
> — Resistance risk is real — never use alone for more than 2 consecutive applications
>
> **FRAC Group 3 (DMI / Triazole):** Propiconazole 14.3, Myclobutanil, Banner Maxx II
> — Systemic, good on dollar spot and spring dead spot
> — Acts slightly as a growth regulator — do not over-apply on bermuda (max 4 fl oz/1,000 sqft/30 days)
>
> **Key rule: Never apply the same FRAC group in two consecutive rounds.**
